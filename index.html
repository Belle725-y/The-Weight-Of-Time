Why this exists

Too many athletes are taught to call harm “normal.”

In top-level sports people often think that being hit, controlled, humiliated and put under a lot of psychological pressure is a sign that someone is really committed. This project is trying to change that way of thinking by doing research, making art, and providing help to people who want to remain anonymous to people in elite sport and the way elite sport handles things, like corporal punishment, emotional control, humiliation and extreme psychological pressure.

Installation concept

Data becomes material.

  • Sleep: cotton, down, light passing through
  • School and homework: dense paper, gray sand, compression
  • Training: metal, broken blade fragments, bearing weight
  • Free time: clear space, resin, breathing room

Anonymous peer support

Athlete Mental Health Pen Pal Group

An environment where athletes from different sports and nations can discuss issues like fear, exhaustion, stress, identity, and healing without having to prove their strength.

What it can be

Anonymous letters, silent meetings, themed discussion rooms, and optional art exchanges.

What it is not

It is not a replacement for therapy, emergency care, or crisis intervention.
